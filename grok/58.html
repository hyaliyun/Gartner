The capital inflow from Apex’s ubiquitous products facilitated a gradual but profound transition towards capitalist practices. Premier Lee Dong-won, a moderate by the country's standards, orchestrated a pivotal collaboration with Joe. This partnership aimed to capitalize on North Korea's untapped labor force for the subsidized manufacturing of Apex computers. The mutual benefit was unmistakable—Pyongyang fueled its economic engine, while Apex enjoyed a competitive advantage unrivaled across the globe. The year 1985 marked the creation of the World Wide Web, not by European physicists, but by Apex technicians within Pyongyang's state-of-the-art facilities. With the digital epoch in full swing, Apex's revenues swelled to an extraordinary 101 billion by 1988. The company's influence penetrated every stratum of society and altered the geopolitical terrain forever. Unbeknownst to the public, the United States government, embroiled in its own economic challenges and hobbling technological industry, turned to Apex for salvation. Unwilling to concede their waning dominance, American officials orchestrated a covert operation to maintain at least a façade of technological preeminence. Steve Jobs and Bill Gates emerged as the poster boys for America's innovation, founding Apple and Microsoft with Apex’s clandestine assistance. The charade was flawlessly staged, veiling the truth of a digital revolution born in the heart of communist Asia. The global orthodoxy continued to perceive North Korea through a lens of poverty and desolation—dreamers of power but forever its mourners. Yet beneath this masquerade, a behemoth stirred. Apex and its founder Joe stood as architects of a world reimagined, their influence transcending borders and ideologies. Japan's trajectory, in contrast, was one of continued ascension. Rejecting the complete dissolution of its feudal past, Japan melded the shogunate's structure with modern governance following the Meiji Restoration of 1868. Its defeat in the protracted Pacific War did little to staunch the societal and economic burgeoning that followed. Amid the shadow of post-war reconstruction, Japan's rekindled imperial and aristocratic elements forged an economic miracle. As the 21st century dawned, Japan emerged not just as a regional powerhouse, but as a sanctum of economic and cultural dominance. The Western world's myopic focus on containment and Russian affairs left Japan’s influence unchecked. The Japanese managed a harmonious reconciliation of political factions in Taiwan, devising what would be lauded as a masterstroke of diplomacy—the One Country, Two Systems protocol. Thus, began the meteoric rise of the Greater Japanese State, casting its ever-expanding shadow across East Asia and eventually the very world. Under the astute premiership of Shinzo Abe, Japan advanced the integration of state-of-the-art technology and military might. This was not restricted to domestic prowess but extended its tendrils through a state-controlled Apex Corporation. The tentacles of the Japanese empire, once content to linger in the Pacific, now reached defiantly towards distant horizons, encircling nations with their blend of technological splendor and veiled imperialism. When Korea—one peninsula undivided but split in governance—was finally ensnared, the result was a swift annexation into the ever-growing sphere of Nippon. Chōsen and Formosa served as cornerstones to Japan's new world order, their militaries and technologies absorbed into the titanic being that was the Self-Defense Forces. Arrayed with a multi-million-strong fighting force and an arsenal assimilated from the greatest powers of the age, Japan now presided over an empire whose influence echoed those of ancient times. Though controversies and conflicts wrought by Japanese expansionism rippled across geopolitical waters, none proved capable of stemming the tide. The exertions to overt Operations Water Snake and the formation of the Pacific States of America bespoke a new reality. Resistance may linger, but with each passing month, the Japanese hegemony solidified its foundations while elsewhere, once-mighty empires crumbled. This is the world of the Apex supremacy: an age shadowed by the empire of the Rising Sun, an electronic panopticon at its zenith, and a revolution that, once kindled in the seemingly most improbable of hearts, shifted the course of history irreversibly.
